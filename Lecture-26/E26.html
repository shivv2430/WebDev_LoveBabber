*Rasugulla*

"Black rasgulla" is generally known as Kala Jamun or Black Gulab Jamun. The dessert gets its signature dark color from frying the milk solids mixture for a longer time until it caramelizes, rather than just a golden brown. The core ingredients usually include khoya (evaporated milk solids), paneer (cottage cheese), and flour.

Ingredients:

For the Jamuns:

  • Khoya (mawa): 250 grams (about 1 cup tightly packed)
  • Paneer (cottage cheese): 100 grams (about Â½ cup tightly packed, grated)
  • All-purpose flour (maida): 3-6 tablespoons
  • Milk (optional, as needed for kneading): 1 tablespoon
  • Oil or ghee (clarified butter) for deep-frying

For the Sugar Syrup:

  • Sugar: 1.5 - 2 cups
  • Water: 2 - 2.5 cups
  • Cardamom pods (slit) or powder: 3-4
  • Saffron strands (optional): A few

Instructions:

Prepare the Sugar Syrup

  1. Combine the sugar, water, cardamom pods, and saffron strands (if using) in a wide, shallow pan.
  2. Boil the mixture, stirring until the sugar dissolves completely. Add a few drops of lemon juice.
  3. Simmer the syrup until it becomes slightly thick (a single-thread consistency is not necessary, but it shouldn't be too watery). Keep it warm while you prepare the jamuns.

Prepare the Jamun Dough

  1. Crumble the khoya and grate the paneer into a large bow
  2. Knead them together well with your hands until the mixture is smooth and soft.
  3. Add the all-purpose flour to the mixture.
  4. Knead to a soft, pliable dough. Add milk a tablespoon at a time if the dough is too dry, but avoid over-kneading. The dough should be smooth and have no cracks.
  5. Shape the dough into small, smooth balls (or an oblong shape). Ensure there are no cracks on the surface, as this can cause them to break while frying.

Fry and Soak the Jamuns

  1. Heat the oil or ghee in a deep pan over medium-low heat. The oil should be hot, but not smoking hot.
  2. Gently add a few balls into the oil, ensuring they have enough space to expand.
  3. Fry the jamuns slowly and patiently, stirring occasionally, until they reach a rich, dark brown, almost black, color. The low heat helps them cook evenly from the inside out and develop the dark exterior through caramelization.
  4. Remove the fried jamuns and place them on a paper towel for a few minutes to cool slightly.
  5. Immediately transfer the warm jamuns into the warm sugar syrup.
  6. Soak the jamuns for at least 2 hours (or longer) so they can absorb the syrup and become soft and spongy.

Garnish with chopped pistachios and serve warm or chilled.

*Barfi*

Barfi (or burfi) is a popular Indian fudge-like sweet made from milk solids and sugar. This recipe is a quick, instant version using milk powder, which can be prepared in about 20 minutes.

Instant Cardamom Barfi Recipe

This recipe yields a rich, creamy barfi that resembles vanilla fudge.

  1. Prep time: 6 minutes
  2. Cook time: 14 minutes
  3. Servings: Varies by cut size

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups full cream milk powder
  • 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup + 3 tablespoons milk
  • 1/2 cup + 3 tablespoons milk
  • 3 tablespoons butter or ghee
  • 1/2 to 1 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • To garnish (optional): Approx. 2 tablespoons slivered pistachios, saffron threads, or edible silver leaf

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the pan: Grease a 9x6 baking pan or tray and line it with parchment paper for easy removal.
  2. Melt the butter/ghee: In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat the butter or ghee and 1/2 cup of milk over medium-low heat until the butter is melted.
  3. Mix ingredients: Once the butter is melted, turn off the heat and gradually add the milk powder, stirring constantly until combined. Gradually add the powdered sugar, a little at a time. If the mixture seems too dry, add the remaining 3 tablespoons of milk, one tablespoon at a time, until it reaches a paste-like consistency.
  4. Cook the mixture: Turn the heat back on to low and stir continuously for 8-9 minutes. Cook until the mixture thickens and starts to pull away from the sides of the pan and looks like a soft dough. Be careful not to overcook, as this can make the barfi hard.
  5. Flavor and set: Turn off the heat and mix in the vanilla extract, ground cardamom, and a tablespoon of pistachios (if using).
  6. Transfer to the pan: Spread the mixture evenly into the greased, lined pan. Top with the remaining pistachios, saffron, or silver leaf, pressing gently to set the garnishes into the barfi.
  7. Cool and cut: Allow the barfi to set completely for at least three hours at room temperature, or refrigerate for an hour until firm. Once set, use a sharp knife to cut it into squares or diamond shapes.

Storage

Store the barfi in an airtight container at room temperature for up to seven days, or refrigerate for 4-5 days.
